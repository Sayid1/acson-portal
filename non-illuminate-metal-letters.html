Quality Frontlit Channel Letters for Businesses

Our indoor and outdoor cut metal letters are available in wide range of metals including aluminum, stainless steel, Cor-Ten steel, copper, brass and bronze and come with a lifetime guarantee. They thickness and height can be customized according to customer requirements.

Metal letters are made two different ways, all of the letters are cut using water jet or laser(also called Flat cut metal letters) except for cast aluminum(also called Injection Molded Plastic Letters).

Metal letters are themselves not illuminated, but lights can be position near or around them if you desire to have them lit. If you are looking for large, illuminated letters, you may want to consider checking out our page on Frontlit channel letters or Backlit channel letters.

Customization Options For Metal Letters And Logos

Our indoor and outdoor cut metal sign letters are available in wide range of metals including aluminum, stainless steel, Cor-Ten steel, copper, brass and bronze and come with a lifetime guarantee. The waterjet letters are cut from solid plate metal using an advanced, precision cutting technique.

  • Aluminum Finishes: Brushed or Primed for Paint, Polished, Paint, Novena Patina, Rust Powder-coat, Clear Anodized, Polished Clear Anodized, Gold Anodized, Polished Gold Anodized, Medium Bronze Anodized, Polished Medium Bronze Anodized, Dark Bronze Anodized, Polished Dark Bronze Anodized, Black Anodized, Polished Black Anodized.
  • Bronze Finishes: Brushed, Polished, Oxidized, Dark Oxidized, Turquoise Patina, Traditional Verde Patina.
  • Steel Finishes: Brushed Stainless, Polished Stainless & Cor-Ten. Alloy C304 is the standard stainless, it is the most versatile and most widely used stainless steel. ( For areas within 50 miles of the coast. We recommend Alloy 316. This conforms to recommendations by numerous metal industry panels. Use of 304 in coastal areas may experience premature failure. Alloy 316 is the standard molybdenum-bearing grade. The molybdenum gives 316 better overall corrosion resistant properties than C304, particularly higher resistance to pitting and crevice corrosion in chloride environments.

Mounting Options for Metal Letters

Plain

Stud Mount

Welded Stud

Double Face Tape

Plain:

Provided without any mounting hardware. For most metal finishes, processing holes will need to be made on the back of the letters.

Welded Stud Mount:

Backs of 1/8” thick letters and Logos are supplied with welded stud bosses to receive studs or 1” long welded studs, for mounting to wall.

Stud Mount:

Backs of letters and Logos, at least 1/4” thick, are drilled and tapped to receive threaded studs, for mounting to wall.

Double Face Tape Mount:

Letters and Logos have strips of clear double face tape applied to the backs of the letters.

What Is the Difference Between Metals?

Aluminum is lightweight, doesn’t rust and has the lowest price of the metals. It is also the most popular. It can be painted any color or left to its natural silver. Notably, there are two types of aluminum letters, those cut out of solid aluminum plate and cast letters formed from molten aluminum poured into molds. The primary difference is depth; cast aluminum letters are hollow on the back, thus making them have a lower price. Also, once the cast aluminum letters are mounted on a surface, the hollow part is completely concealed.

After aluminum stainless steel is the most popular option. Third most popular is bronze and then brass. Bronze and brass can be buffed or polished to provide a pleasing mirror-like finish. Over time, bronze will gradually gain a greenish film.
